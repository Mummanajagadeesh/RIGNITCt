ORIGO'25

Annual Robotics Workshop — Robotics Interest Group (RIG), NIT Calicut

ORIGO is an annual robotics workshop by the Robotics Interest Group (RIG) at NIT Calicut. It introduces freshmen to the foundations of robotics — electronics, CAD, PCB design, microcontrollers, mechanics, programming, and AI. Sessions are fully hands-on and focus on skill-building.

ORIGO Robotics Challenge

An individual challenge - complete it before 15 November using what you have learned from the ORIGO workshop.

Eligibility

Open to all ORIGO workshop participants. This is an individual-only event - no teams allowed.

Choose Your Project Focus

Select any one of the categories below and build something original around it.

CAD
Programming
AI / YOLO / MediaPipe
PCB Design
Electronics Simulation

Judging Criteria

Submissions will be evaluated based on:

Effort
Originality
Execution

How to Participate

Step 1 - Build

Design or create your project in your chosen category using your skills from ORIGO sessions.

Step 2 - Document

Record a short video (30-120 seconds) showing what you built, how it works, and what inspired it.

Step 3 - Submit

Submit through any one of the approved methods below before the deadline.

Submission Methods

Instagram

Post your video and caption, tag @rig_nitc, and make your post public.

LinkedIn

Upload your post on LinkedIn tagging Robotics Interest Group (RIG NITC) with a brief project description.

Offline

Submit your video and short write-up in person at the Mechatronics Lab (same building as CAD CAM Lab).

Deadline: 14 November, 5:00 PM

Rewards

One winner will be chosen from each category. Winners receive an exclusive Robotics Kit containing:

  • • Differential chassis
  • • NodeMCU (ESP8266) controller
  • • L293D motor driver
  • • DC motors × 2
  • • Battery pack + 4×AA cells
  • • Breadboard & jumper wires
  • • Screwdriver & phone stand
Keep experimenting. Keep building.
We will see you and your creations on 15 November for the final hands-on day.
